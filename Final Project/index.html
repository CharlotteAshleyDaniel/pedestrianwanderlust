ABOUT

Pedestrian Wanderlust is a video portrait series of improvised dance in public spaces. The project focuses on how dancers interact with the unknown in their surrounding environments and aims to give us an inside look at their creative process in action. In each film we get to know the dancers not just for their physicality, but also their choices, curiosities, and response to the world and people around them. The resulting footage provides us with a glimpse of the individual’s unique movement identity; a representation of their internal groove brought to life as we watch them respond through movement to the various stimuli they find at the locations they choose to explore. Before the improvisation, a starting and ending location are agreed upon and we see the reactions of other pedestrians as the dancer takes themselves on a 5-10 minute journey shot in a single uninterrupted take then sped up to emphasize the overall trajectory without exhausting the viewer with a long run time. All films are shot on an iPhone6 with natural light in a duet between dancer and videographer. Moving often times in silence, the dancers respond to their own rhythms and music is selected and added afterwards. I’m honored to have the opportunity to celebrate a diversity of movement and to capture the intimate experiences that these dancers simultaneously live and create for themselves. Such a dance is life and the humans I have had the pleasure of working with so far have been breathtakingly gorgeous, courageous, and authentic. I have fallen in a certain kind of love with each and every one of them throughout this process.

~Rami Shafi Founder and Director
